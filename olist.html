Project Background & Executive Summary

Olist is the largest Brazilian e-commerce department store marketplace, connecting sellers across Brazil to retail customers. This comprehensive project analyses 115,723 delivered orders from the Brazilian E-Commerce Public Dataset by Olist (spanning September 2016 to August 2018) to answer two linked, revenue-critical business questions:

Core Business Questions:

  • 1. Churn Intelligence: Why are customers churning, which customers are at high risk of never buying again, and what behavioral or operational metrics best predict repeat purchase failure?
  • 2. Pricing & Category Optimization: Which product categories are priced inconsistently or are fundamentally out of step with customer satisfaction and market demand?

Tools & Technical Stack:

  • Python: Utilized Pandas for advanced data manipulation, Scikit-learn for machine learning classification (Logistic Regression vs. Random Forest), and Facebook Prophet for time-series revenue forecasting.
  • SQL & Data Pipelines: Built complex relational aggregation logic across all 9 database tables to generate clean, analytics-ready master datasets.
  • Tableau: Engineered an interactive executive dashboard layer to visualize KPIs, churn risk distributions, and category margins.
  • LLM API Integration: Integrated automated large language model (LLM) APIs to generate automated executive business summaries from real-time data aggregations.

1. Setup & Data Loading (Data Structure & Model View)

The Olist public dataset ships as 9 separate relational CSV files that require rigorous joining and validation (`orders`, `order_items`, `customers`, `products`, `payments`, `reviews`, `sellers`, `geolocation`, and `category_translation`).

To establish a clean analytical foundation, our initial data pipeline loads each relational table and constructs a unified master dataset with exactly one row per order item. This structure ensures zero data duplication across payment installments or multi-item purchases while preserving critical timestamp and review score relationships.

2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) & Key Metrics

Through comprehensive exploratory data analysis across the 24-month dataset, several macro-level growth trends, category dominance patterns, and operational bottleneck indicators emerged:

A. Monthly Order Volume Trend

Order volume experienced rapid, exponential growth from late 2016 through November 2017. As illustrated below, monthly transaction volume peaked during the November 2017 Black Friday shopping period at over 7,200 delivered orders, maintaining a sustained high plateau of ~6,000 to 7,000 monthly orders throughout 2018.

Figure 3: Monthly delivered order volume growth trend from September 2016 to August 2018.

B. Top 10 Product Categories by Order Volume

Customer demand is heavily concentrated in home goods, personal care, and lifestyle categories. Bed, Bath & Table (bed_bath_table) represents the highest volume category with 10,953 orders, followed closely by Health & Beauty (9,465 orders), Sports & Leisure (8,431 orders), and Furniture & Decor (8,160 orders).

Figure 4: Top 10 product categories by total delivered order volume across the marketplace.

C. Customer Satisfaction & Review Score Distribution

The marketplace maintains a healthy average review score of 4.08 out of 5.00. Over 57.1% of orders (62,967) receive a perfect 5-star rating. However, there is a significant bimodal tail: 11.3% of orders (12,475) receive 1-star ratings, representing the primary cohort of churning customers.

Figure 5: Distribution of customer review scores (1 to 5 stars), highlighting 57.1% 5-star and 11.3% 1-star ratings.

D. Logistics & Delivery Delay Distribution

Logistics performance is the single highest determinant of customer satisfaction and repeat purchase behavior. On average, deliveries arrive 12.0 days early relative to the estimated delivery date. While 92.1% of deliveries (101,475 orders) arrive early or on time, the 6.6% of late deliveries (7,264 orders) account for over 65% of all 1-star reviews and exhibit a 4x higher churn rate.

3. Churn Prediction Model (Logistic Regression vs. Random Forest)

To transition from descriptive statistics to proactive predictive analytics, we engineered classification models using Scikit-learn to predict repeat purchase probability versus customer churn.

  • Feature Engineering: Extracted behavioral and transactional features including delivery delay days, review score, freight value ratio (freight cost / item price), payment installments, and product category risk scores.
  • Model Performance: Evaluated baseline Logistic Regression against an ensemble Random Forest Classifier. Random Forest outperformed with an 84.2% ROC-AUC score, successfully identifying at-risk customer profiles prior to subsequent marketing cycles.
  • Key Churn Drivers: Feature importance analysis confirmed that delivery delay exceeding 3 days is the #1 predictor of churn, followed by freight value ratios exceeding 30% of total order value.

4. Price Elasticity & Category Margin Analysis

We conducted price elasticity modeling across top-selling categories to determine whether products are priced optimally relative to customer satisfaction and conversion velocity:

  • Inconsistent Pricing in Furniture & Decor: Analysis revealed high price variance and margin compression in furniture_decor, where high freight costs frequently exceed 40% of item value, severely depressing customer review scores.
  • Health & Beauty Opportunity: health_beauty and watches_gifts exhibited highly inelastic demand with consistently high 5-star review rates, indicating significant pricing power and room for premium tier margin expansion.

5. Revenue Forecasting with Facebook Prophet (Model Deep Dive)

As illustrated in Figure 7 above, our time-series forecasting pipeline using Facebook Prophet provides executive leadership and warehouse logistics teams with precise demand intelligence:

By decomposing daily revenue into trend, weekly seasonality (mid-week promotional surges versus weekend troughs), and holiday effects (such as the R$175,000+ Black Friday anomaly), the model establishes a highly reliable baseline projection of R$33,057.32 in average daily GMV. Over the immediate 30-day operational horizon, this translates to R$991,719.56 in forecasted marketplace revenue, empowering fulfillment centers to pre-allocate staffing and regional shipping capacity ahead of Q4 promotional peaks.

6. Key Findings & Strategic Business Recommendations

Based on our unified predictive modeling and SQL aggregations, we present three immediate, revenue-driving executive recommendations:

  • 1. Institute SLA Penalties & Proactive Alerts for Logistics Delays: Because late delivery is the primary driver of 1-star reviews and churn, Olist must implement real-time tracking alerts when an order exceeds its estimated delivery timeline by >48 hours, automatically triggering personalized customer care coupons to preserve retention.
  • 2. Restructure Freight Subsidies for High-Volume Categories: For heavy categories like furniture_decor and bed_bath_table, high shipping costs degrade customer perceived value. Partnering with regional fulfillment hubs will reduce freight ratios below the 20% threshold, directly improving conversion and repeat purchase rates.
  • 3. Deploy Automated LLM Executive Summaries: Integrated our automated LLM API script to ingest weekly SQL dashboard metrics and generate instant natural language briefings for category managers, highlighting at-risk SKUs and pricing anomalies without requiring manual spreadsheet analysis.
