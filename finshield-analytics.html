In financial institutions and payment gateways, fraud detection cannot wait for nightly batch jobs. If a bad actor attempts to drain a compromised account or route funds through a money mule ring, the system must intercept and freeze the transaction right inside the synchronous payment authorization loop.

FinShield-AI bridges the gap between high-speed Data Analytics (SQL, dbt, DuckDB) and Full-Stack AI Engineering (XGBoost, NetworkX Graph Analytics, SHAP Explainability, FastAPI), achieving sub-50 millisecond synchronous inference.

1. Multi-Page Platform Navigation & BI Analytics Engine

The FinShield-AI v1.0.0 PROD application is structured into three specialized analytical intelligence modules designed for financial crime investigators, compliance officers, and data engineering teams:

  • BI Analytics & SQL Explorer: An interactive, sub-second exploratory analytics interface powered by an embedded DuckDB & dbt Engine evaluating multi-million transaction records.
  • Graph ML & Money Mule Tracker: Advanced topological network analysis powered by NetworkX to identify multi-hop money mule rings and cyclic fund routing behavior across accounts.
  • Live Simulator & Explainable AI (SHAP): Real-time transaction interception suite with instant mathematical feature attributions (`SHAPContribution`) to explain model decisions before funds settle.

Initial Page: BI Analytics & High-Speed SQL Explorer

When entering the platform, investigators land directly on the BI Analytics & SQL Explorer dashboard. Utilizing DuckDB's in-memory columnar execution engine, the dashboard processes and aggregates 6,362,620 transactions (`$1144.39B` total volume) with zero latency.

Key Operational KPIs & Anomaly Detections:

  • Confirmed Fraud Incidence (`8,213 Cases`): Isolates verified illicit transfers across the dataset (`0.129%` global fraud rate).
  • Balance Drain Anomalies (`8,024 Accounts`): Automatically flags transactions where account balances are instantaneously drained to absolute zero (`newbalanceOrig = 0`), a primary signature of account takeover (ATO) liquidations.
  • Money Mule Ring Hops (`0 Verified Sequences`): Monitors multi-account chaining across the active slice.

Interactive Slicers & DuckDB Aggregations:

To investigate specific time windows without waiting for slow database re-queries, the dashboard provides dynamic slicers spanning all 743 evaluated steps (hourly intervals):

  • Temporal Slicing (`All 30 Days`, `First 7 Days`, `Top 15 Fraud Spikes`): Instantly filters the underlying transactional timeline (`step`), isolating high-risk windows and anomaly clusters.
  • Step-by-Step Volume & Fraud Rate Timeline: Displays sliced transaction counts against exact fraud incidence percentages (`%`) across every individual hour.
  • 24-Hour Time-of-Day Behavioral Risk Profile: Maps historical fraud volume against the 24-hour daily cycle (`step % 24`), exposing critical behavioral vulnerability windows during early morning and late-night settlement periods.

2. Topological Graph ML & Money Mule Ring Tracker

Switching from the SQL Explorer to the Graph ML & Money Mule Tracker module transitions the platform from columnar tabular aggregation to directed graph topological intelligence. In sophisticated financial crimes, bad actors rarely transfer stolen funds directly to their ultimate withdrawal destination; instead, they route money through complex networks of intermediary shell accounts ("money mules") to break standard SQL audit trails.

Powered by NetworkX graph traversal algorithms, this module automatically constructs directed transactional subgraphs (`G = (V, E)` where accounts represent vertices and fund flows represent weighted edges). By evaluating topological properties—such as PageRank centrality, betweenness hub scores, and multi-hop cycle detection (`A -> B -> C -> A`)—the platform exposes coordinated syndicates and hidden money mule rings in real time.

Figure 5: FinShield-AI v1.0.0 PROD — Graph ML & Money Mule Tracker module isolating directed transaction networks and topological anomalies.

Interactive Case Inspection: Deep-Dive Ring Traversal

This granular case inspection mode reveals exact node-to-node transaction timestamps, transfer velocity amounts (`amount`), and intermediate account balances, empowering compliance officers to trace illicit fund movements from initial compromise to ultimate exit without manual graph reconstruction.

3. Real-Time Transaction Interception & Explainable AI (SHAP)

Switching to the Live Simulator & Explainable AI (SHAP) module shifts the platform from historical slice inspection to live, synchronous transaction interception. Under strict institutional regulatory mandates, black-box machine learning models—which output a raw risk probability without justifying the underlying rationale—cannot be safely deployed for automated payment freezing.

To solve this critical compliance bottleneck, FinShield-AI embeds exact mathematical explainability right into the inference pipeline (`sub-50ms execution window`). Whenever a live transaction is intercepted and scored above the fraud threshold (`e.g., risk probability > 85%`), the system simultaneously calculates real-time SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) attributions (`SHAPContribution`).

The interactive attribution chart breaks down the exact positive and negative feature drivers (`amount`, `oldbalanceOrg`, `newbalanceOrig`, `step % 24`), showing compliance officers and audit committees precisely *why* the AI flagged the transaction before funds leave the financial institution.

Figure 7: FinShield-AI v1.0.0 PROD — Live Simulator & Explainable AI module displaying real-time transaction evaluation and exact SHAP feature attributions.

Selecting the Money Mule Velocity filter injects high-frequency, multi-account rapid transfer sequences (`rapid account ingress followed immediately by full-balance egress within seconds`) into the active inference loop. As shown below, the temporal XGBoost classifier immediately intercepts the high-velocity sequence, while the SHAP explainer dynamically shifts its primary attribution drivers (`SHAPContribution`) to highlight transfer frequency anomalies and rapid balance drain signatures.

An institutional fraud detection engine must achieve strict precision balances: while intercepting sophisticated crime, it must minimize false positives that freeze routine customer payments and degrade operational trust.

By selecting the Legitimate behavioral filter, compliance engineers can inspect routine baseline transactions (`e.g., standard merchant purchases, recurring payroll transfers, and normal account balance ratios`). As shown below, the temporal classifier immediately clears the transaction with minimal risk probability (`< 5%`), while the SHAP attribution chart highlights negative contribution drivers—confirming that stable balance ratios and routine temporal intervals actively drive down the fraud score.
